What Makes A Good Web Design

What makes a good web design that actually works for your business? While everyone has an opinion (like whether a hot dog is a sandwich or not), the research is clear, functionality makes a huge impact. In fact, 42% of people leave a website because it's cluttered or confusing. We've all landed on those sites that look expensive but make it impossible to find what you need. You’re probably not selling Lamborghini's or Rolex watches, so let’s build a website that actually reflects what you provide. That's why we prioritize simple designs that provide clear communication about the services and/or products you provide. Our goal is to create a website that's easy to use, accessible to all visitors, and reflects your unique brand without the clutter that drives customers away.

What Should a Good Design Cost?

Finding a good design can be challenging enough — figuring out what you should pay for it is even harder. Let's break down what actually goes into building a quality website so you understand where your investment goes.

  1. What Goes Into Building a High Quality Website:
  2. Design: A professional layout that fits all your business information and looks great.
  3. Copywriting: Well-written content that speaks to your customers and clearly explains what you do.
  4. Development: The technical work of coding your site using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, (the languages that browsers use to read a website).
  5. Hosting: Server space that keeps your website live and accessible 24/7.
  6. Domain Name: Your internet address (like harvestclicks.com) that customers use to find you.

Each of these pieces requires different skills and time, which is where most of the cost comes from. There are also ongoing expenses for hosting and domain registration.
