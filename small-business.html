Why We focus on Small Businesses

Small businesses are the backbone of the American economy. According to the U.S. Chamber of Commerce, over 99% of businesses in the U.S. are classified as small businesses by the SBA (less than 500 employees and privately owned). Even more importantly, they're the country's biggest employer, nearly half of all Americans work for a small business.

That's the world I grew up in. My dad was a farmer, and my grandfather owned a small construction company. I worked for both of them as a teenager, and for many other small businesses as I started my career. I've seen firsthand the challenges business owners face: the late nights, the uncertainty, and the constant pressure to adapt in a rapidly changing world, especially with technology advancing faster than ever.

Small business owners are the unsung heroes of our communities, yet they're often under-served by the tech industry. Many tech companies over complicate things with jargon, and overpriced packages. These are the customers I want to serve, hardworking people who deserve straightforward, honest help with their websites and online presence.

Do I Really Need a Website?

In today's market, a quality website isn't optional, it's essential to stay competitive. As Millennials and Gen Z become the majority of consumers, their shopping habits are reshaping how businesses need to market themselves. According to Semrush, 74% of Gen Z shoppers prefer to shop on a mobile device, and similar trends hold true for Millennials and Gen X.

Think about what that means for your business: if potential customers can't find you online, especially on their phone, they're moving on to your competitor who does have a website. It's that simple.

Your grandparents might have run a successful business without a website, but the game has changed. Today's buyers research products and services online before making any purchasing decision. They're comparing options, reading reviews, and looking for businesses they can trust, all from their phones. If you're not showing up in those searches with a professional online presence, you're invisible to a huge portion of your potential customer base.

The good news? Getting online doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. Now is the time to showcase what your business does well to these information-hungry buyers who are ready to hire someone like you.

Let's Talk About AI

Whether we like it or not, Artificial Intelligence is here, and it's already changing how business is done. No one knows exactly what the world will look like in 5 to 10 years, but we do know that AI will continue to bring innovations that reshape how customers find and choose businesses. Staying alert and ready to adapt is essential if you want to take advantage of these new tools.

Change is challenging, but it also brings opportunity. You have a business to run and probably don't have time to stay up-to-date on AI developments, but you also don't want to be left behind if there's a better way for customers to find you. That's where we come in as your partner. We make it our job to learn the latest technology so we can better serve busy business owners like you.

The way people search for businesses is already evolving. Voice search, AI assistants, and new search tools are changing customer behavior, which means SEO strategies need to evolve too. It's still early, and the landscape is shifting quickly, but we're committed to staying ahead of these changes so your business stays competitive.

Here's the reality: if you're hesitant to adapt, your competitors might not be. Let us handle the tech side so you can focus on what you do best — running your business.
